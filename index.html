INFO

The purpose of this course is to provide an understanding of how data and statistical analysis can improve managerial decision-making. Students will learn how to ask well-posed questions, collect or find relevant data, and apply appropriate statistical methods to solve problems and make better business and policy decisions. Together, we will explore statistical methods for gleaning insights from economic and social data, with an emphasis on approaches to identifying casual relationships. We will also discuss how to design and analyze randomized experiments and introduce econometric methods for estimating casual effects in non-experimental data.

Satisfies: 2 units; the statistics req. for admission into Haas (taken in conjunction with Data 8)
Prerequisites: concurrent enrollment in Data 8 or previous completion of Data 8 and one semester of calculus (not strictly enforced, but highly recommended)
Skills you'll gain: further experience with JupyterHub (a tool growing in popularity), comfort with interpreting and analyzing economic and social data (using Python in particular), knowing when and how to apply standard casual inference methods
